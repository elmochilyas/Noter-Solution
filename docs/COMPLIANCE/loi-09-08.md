# Loi 09-08 — Moroccan Personal Data Protection

## Scope

Loi 09-08 is Morocco's personal data protection law. It applies to any processing of personal data of individuals located in Morocco, regardless of where the processor is based.

**This site processes personal data of Moroccan residents.** Compliance is mandatory.

This document is engineering-facing: it translates legal obligations into concrete system requirements. It is **not** legal advice — Sana's legal review (or an outside counsel) must sign off before launch.

## Regulator

**CNDP** — Commission Nationale de Contrôle de la Protection des Données à Caractère Personnel.

- Website: cndp.ma
- Notifications and authorizations submitted through their portal.
- Inspection authority — can audit, fine, order suspension of processing.

## Lawful basis

For each data category, document the basis:

| Data | Basis |
|---|---|
| Identity (name, email, phone) | Contractual necessity — without it, no service |
| Uploaded documents | Contractual necessity — required for the consultation |
| CIN number (if requested) | Legal obligation (notarial professional rules) |
| Payment data | Contractual necessity + legal obligation (fiscal record-keeping) |
| Chatbot conversations | Legitimate interest (service improvement) — opt-out available |
| IP address, user agent (logs) | Legitimate interest (security) |
| Notification preferences | Consent |

## Data subject rights

A client can request:

| Right | How to exercise | Response time |
|---|---|---|
| Access | Email to sana.bouhamidi@gmail.com or via portal | 30 days |
| Rectification | Via portal (profile section) or email | 30 days |
| Erasure | Via portal "Delete account" or email | 30 days |
| Restriction of processing | Email | 30 days |
| Opposition (legitimate-interest processing only) | Email | 30 days |
| Withdraw consent | Via portal preferences | Immediate |

### Erasure mechanics

See `ARCHITECTURE/auth.md` → "Client account deletion" for the anonymization process.

Key principle: hard deletion would break referential integrity with legal records (bookings, payments, receipts retained 10 years). We anonymize the Client row instead — the consultation history remains, but the individual is no longer identifiable.

Documents older than 90 days post-appointment are already auto-purged, so erasure mainly applies to the Client profile and conversation logs.

### Access request response format

When a client requests access, generate a single ZIP containing:

- A PDF summary of all data we hold (profile, bookings, payments, conversations, notifications log filtered to them).
- A CSV export of structured records.
- Copies of any documents they uploaded that haven't been purged.

Sana or the assistant generates this via Filament admin → Client detail page → "Export client data" action.

## Cross-border data transfers

Loi 09-08 restricts transfers outside Morocco. Permitted destinations:

- Countries with equivalent protection (CNDP-approved list — currently mostly EU)
- With CNDP authorization for specific transfers
- Based on explicit consent for that transfer

Our transfers:

| Recipient | Location | Data | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Supabase | EU (Frankfurt) | All app data | EU-equivalent protection |
| Stripe | Ireland (EU) | Payment data only | EU + Stripe's compliance posture |
| Twilio | Ireland (EU) | Phone numbers, message bodies | EU |
| Resend | EU region | Email addresses, email bodies | EU |
| Cerebras | USA | Chatbot messages (user input + retrieved FAQ context) | Explicit consent in chatbot disclaimer + no PII included |

### Cerebras transfer specifics

The US transfer is the riskiest. Mitigations:

- Chatbot disclaimer (shown before first message and accepted) explicitly mentions US processing.
- System prompt instructs users not to share PII.
- We don't send user identity (no email / phone / name) to the API.
- We only send the conversation messages and retrieved FAQ excerpts.
- ⚠️ Cerebras data processing region: **to be confirmed with provider** (Cerebras is headquartered in Sunnyvale, CA; no published data residency guarantees found in public documentation). A DPA should be requested from Cerebras before production launch.

This is the right-sized risk for the value of having a chatbot. If CNDP raises objection, switch to a Morocco-/EU-hosted LLM provider (this would be a v2 effort).

## Cookie policy

We use:

| Cookie | Purpose | Type | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| `XSRF-TOKEN` | CSRF protection | Strictly necessary | Session |
| `sana_session` | Session ID | Strictly necessary | 2 hours (client) / 30 min (admin) |
| `locale` | Language preference | Functional (consent presumed via use) | 1 year |
| `verified_device` | 2FA device recognition (admin only) | Strictly necessary | 30 days |

No third-party advertising cookies. No tracking cookies. Analytics is cookie-free (Plausible).

Cookie banner: minimal, informative banner on first visit linking to the privacy notice. Not a consent banner because we don't use anything requiring consent — but presenting the info is good practice.

## Sanctions

- Administrative fines up to 300,000 MAD per violation.
- Criminal penalties possible for severe violations.
- Order to stop processing.

Risk-adjusted, our exposure is modest because we're not in marketing data, ad tech, or large-scale processing. But the receipt-retention obligation, breach-notification timelines, and CNDP declarations are non-negotiable.
